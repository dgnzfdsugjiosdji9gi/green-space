How park population become nearly extinct

Park populations become nearly extinct primarily due to human activities which lead to major threats such as:

  • Habitat loss
  • Pollution
  • Climate changes
  • Human disturbance

How can we revitalized our local park?

Clean Up & Fix Up

Picking up litter and fixing broken equipment makes the park safer, more pleasant, and encourages people to respect the space.

Greening the Park

Greenery improves air quality, provides shade, attracts birds and butterflies, and makes the park visually appealing.

Better Facilities

Benches give people a place to rest, bins reduce litter, signs provide guidance or education, and walking paths make the park more usable for everyone.

Proposed Solutions

Adding Recycling Bins

Providing recycling bins helps visitors separate waste properly. Different colored bins can be used for plastic, paper, and general trash, making it easier for everyone to understand.

Awareness Campaigns

Awareness activities help people understand how their actions affect the environment. This includes posters, social media posts, school presentations, and community events that teach visitors the importance of reducing waste.
